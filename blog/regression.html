1. Linear Regression

Math Behind It:

Equation: \( y = \beta_0 + \beta_1x + \epsilon \)

Here:

  • \( y \) is the dependent variable (target).
  • \( x \) is the independent variable (predictor).
  • \( \beta_0 \) is the intercept.
  • \( \beta_1 \) is the slope coefficient.
  • \( \epsilon \) is the error term.

Uses: Linear regression is widely used in predictive analysis and is the foundation of many complex models.

Pros: Simple to implement, interpretable, and works well when the relationship between variables is linear.

Cons: Performs poorly when relationships are non-linear and is sensitive to outliers.
